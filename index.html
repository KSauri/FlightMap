What is the A* Pathfinding Algorithm?

Computers need to find paths often - from maps to video games. How do they do it? One solution is the A* algorithm. It starts at one point and uses two metrics to determine which points to 'consider'. First - how far has it traveled from the origin to get to that next point? Second - how far is that next point from the destination?

You can see this in the visualization! At each turn, the algorithm is considering the best current airport to fly to next. Once chosen, it brings in all the new airports it can fly to, and picks the best among those. Voila! Now you have a quick pathfinding algorithm!
